For parents & families

Learning happens at home, too

Homework support, school breaks, a rainy Sunday: there is always a good moment to learn through play — with paper, pencil and your company.

  • Simple instructions that are easy to follow along
  • Focused, screen-free moments
  • Gentle practice of reading, writing and math
  • Small study rituals that grow into habits
  • Quality time together — the worksheet is just the start of the conversation

These activities complement school — they do not replace teachers.

See activities for home

Learning together is the secret

You do not need to be an expert to follow along: instructions are short and direct, written for the child. Pick an activity, print it together and let curiosity lead. When it is done, it deserves a spot on the fridge.

Paper & pencil

Screen off. Curiosity on.

A printed page invites a child to write, draw, cut, color — and proudly show the result. It is hands-on focus, with room for grown-ups to join in.

  • Handwriting at the right size for small hands
  • Real coloring, cutting and manipulating
  • Side-by-side learning you can be part of
  • Keep the sheets and watch progress over time
